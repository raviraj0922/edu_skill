Coursework:

Module 1: Foundations of Retail & Customer Service

  • Introduction to the position of cashier and the retail industry.
  • Operations of the Point-of-Sale (POS) system: scanning, accepting payments, and printing receipts.
  • Procedures for handling cash include security measures, counting methods, and spotting counterfeit bills.
  • Outstanding client service: rapport-building, communication, and dispute resolution.
  • Both de-escalation tactics and active listening.

Module 2: Retail Technology and Automation

  • An introduction to scanners and barcode technologies.
  • Helping customers and debugging self-checkout systems.
  • Processing and security aspects of mobile wallets and contactless payments.
  • Product information and stock levels are understood by inventory management systems.
  • POS processes using touchscreens and tablets.

Module 3: Retail Innovation and Its Future

  • Omnichannel retail: knowing the connections between online and offline channels.
  • Techniques that involve click-and-collect and customer assistance.
  • The emergence of cashierless stores and self-service kiosks is an example of automation.
  • Understanding consumer behavior and trends through data analytics in retail.
  • Accepting new technology, such as loyalty programs, cashless transactions, and AR/VR apps.

Module 4: Soft Skills for Success in Retail

  • Working together as a team in a hectic setting.
  • Effective work prioritization and time management.
  • Problem-solving and adaptability in changing circumstances.
  • The significance of upholding a pleasant attitude and professionalism.
  • Good communication with supervisors and coworkers.

Final Assessment:
  • MCQ Based Online Assessment 120 Questions (180 Minutes)
  • Realistic evaluations of POS systems and typical checkout situations.
  • Practice customer service skills through role-playing exercises.
  • Written assessments including novel techniques and retail principles.
